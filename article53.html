By Jackie Whetzel

A CALIFORNIA man has experienced one of the luckiest nights of his life at a Las Vegas casino.

He walked into a casino on the Las Vegas strip, made a $5 bet, and left with over $10 million.

It's every gambler's dream.

Most Vegas visitors are happy to leave with more money than they came with.

However, this guy scored the ultimate prize.

The man won the money playing a slot machine in Aria Resort and Casino, according to local ABC affiliate KTNV.

He was playing the Megabucks Gold Forge game.

The man made $5 bets on the slot machine, until he won $10.1 million, according to lottery officials.

The odds of winning a jackpot that big is slim.

There have only been 20 other people who have become multi-millionaires after playing the Megabucks slot machines, according to Onlinegambling.com.

The machines were first installed in 2005.

While this man has opted to keep his identity private for now, a woman named Brittany from Texas came forward in September after she scored her own big jackpot in Vegas.

Brittany was playing a Wheel of Fortune slot machine in Terminal one of the Las Vegas Harry Reid International Airport when she won $643,435.

The airport shared a picture of Brittany standing next to the lucky machine after she hit the jackpot.

"Congrats to Brittany, from Texas, who raked in $643,435.00 playing Wheel of Fortune in the A Gates. Will YOU be the next winner?!" the airport wrote on X.
